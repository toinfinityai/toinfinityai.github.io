FitRep

A synthetic open-source dataset for remote fitness and remote PT applications.
The dataset we wish we had when we were building vision algorithms for fitness.

OVERVIEW

The perfect vision dataset for fitness and PT applications

FitRep makes it easier for engineers to build vision-based products in remote fitness and PT.

The dataset contains 1,000 videos of diverse avatars doing multiple reps 10 common exercises. It includes massive variation in the environment, lighting conditions, avatar demographics, and movement trajectories. From cadence to kinematic trajectory, each rep is done slightly differently (just like real humans would).

FitRep was designed to look just like real-world videos collected by remote exercise companies and therefore integrate seamlessly into existing pipelines. FitRep videos are accompanied by a rich set of pixel-perfect labels and annotations, including rep counts. The data allows engineers to build rep counting models, pose estimation models, form-correction models, segmentation models, activity classification models, and much more.


It features:

  • 1,000 videos of 10-20s each
  • 10 exercises
  • Over 10,000 reps (no two reps are the same)
  • 50 indoor home scenes
  • Full spectrum of lighting conditions (daytime to nighttime)
  • Varied demographics, including body shape, skin tones, and clothing
  • 25 label and annotation types
  • Available for both academic and commercial use

DATASET FEATURES

Infinite Movement Variation

Multiple exercises to select.

The dataset includes 10 of the most common fitness and physical therapy (PT) exercises.

Or pick a new movement altogether.

If none of the exercises are what you’re looking for, get in touch. We can import any movement into simulation and generate synthetic videos for it. You can either leverage your own Mo-Cap data or just tell us the name of the exercise and we’ll take care of the rest.

Each rep is done slightly differently.
Just like a real human.

From cadence variation to varying kinematic trajectories, no two reps are done exactly the same way. We built in vast amounts of movement variation because robust algorithms need robust variation in the training data.

Transitions and breaks.
Perfect labels to help solve the hard stuff.

The avatars transition into and out of the exercises and periodically take breaks in the middle. We know that this is the hard stuff to build robust algorithms around, so we made sure to provide perfect rep count and activity classification labels.

Any room.
Any camera angle.
Any lighting condition.

The dataset includes 50 different home settings with a range of lighting conditions and camera angles.

Diverse avatars for equitable and fair ML.

Give everyone a great home fitness experience by designing for a range of skin tones, body types, and clothing. The dataset has a normal distribution of body-types and equal distribution of skin tones.

Build without fear.

FitRep is one of the few human-centric datasets that is available for both academic and commercial use.
