Synthetic data. Real results.

InfiniteRep makes it easier for engineers to build vision-based products in remote fitness and PT.

Designed to look like real-world videos collected by remote exercise companies, the InfiniteRep dataset seamlessly integrates into existing pipelines. Each video is accompanied by a rich set of pixel-perfect labels and annotations, including rep counts.


It features:

  • 1,000 videos (5-30 seconds each)
  • 10 exercises
  • Lifelike rep behavior
  • 7 indoor scenes
  • Diverse lighting conditions
  • Varied avatar body shape, skin tones, and clothing
  • 18 label and annotation types

DATASET FEATURES

Lifelike rep behavior

Each rep is done slightly differently.
Just like a real human.

From cadence variation to varying kinematic trajectories, no two reps are done exactly the same way. We built in vast amounts of movement variation to enable you to train robust algorithms.

Inspired by real people.

We used extensive real world movement data to model the unique intricacies of movement and produce realistic and robust training data.

Robust exercise library

Choose from common exercises.

The dataset includes 10 of the most common fitness and physical therapy (PT) exercises.

Or import any movement.

Generate synthetic videos from any movement. Leverage your own motion capture (Mo-Cap) data or just tell us the name of the exercise and we’ll take care of the rest.

Infinite avatars and environments

Varied rooms, camera angles, and lighting conditions.

The dataset includes 7 backgrounds of home settings with a range of lighting conditions and camera angles.

Diverse avatars for equitable and fair ML.

Give everyone a great home fitness experience by designing for a range of skin tones, body types, and clothing. The dataset has a normal distribution of body-types and equal distribution of skin tones.

Detailed labels

Pixel-perfect labels and rich annotations.

The dataset allows engineers to build rep counting models, pose estimation models, form-correction models, segmentation models, activity classification models, and much more.

Open-source

Build without limits.

InfiniteRep is one of the few human-centric datasets that is available for both academic and commercial use.
